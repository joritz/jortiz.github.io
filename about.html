About Pugs

Pugs are a toy breed that originated in China over 2,000 years ago. They were originally bred as companion dogs for Chinese emperors and were treasured by royal families. Pugs eventually made their way to Europe, where they became a popular breed among European royalty as well. In particular, they were beloved by the Dutch royal family, and the breed spread across Europe, becoming well-known for their playful and affectionate nature.

Pugs are small in size, typically weighing between 14 and 18 pounds, with a sturdy and muscular build. They have a distinct look with their wrinkled faces, large eyes, and short snouts. Their tails curl tightly over their backs, adding to their unique charm. While their appearance is unmistakable, it's their personality that makes them truly special. Pugs are known for being clownish and entertaining; they enjoy making people laugh and are generally very social and happy dogs.

Their temperament makes them ideal for families, as they are gentle and friendly with both adults and children. Pugs are often called “shadows” because they like to follow their owners everywhere, showing how deeply bonded they are to their families. Although they are not overly energetic, they do enjoy playing and can have bursts of excitement. Overall, pugs are affectionate, loyal, and devoted companions who bring joy to everyone around them.
